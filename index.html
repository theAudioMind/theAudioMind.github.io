AudioMind: MLLM Reasoning and Preference Learning for Video-to-Audio Generation

Anonymous Authors

Abstract. Video-text-to-audio (VT2A) generation aims to synthesize realistic audio conditioned on video and text, yet two persistent challenges remain: semantic alignment--reasoning from acoustic hints and following structured audio-visual relation descriptions; and temporal alignment--achieving robust audio-visual synchronization. We propose AudioMind, a generative model that addresses these challenges through multimodal large language model (MLLM) reasoning and preference learning. For semantic understanding, we construct VGGSound-Think, a tri-modal dataset that augments VGGSound with acoustic hints and structured audio-visual relation annotations, and leverage an MLLM backbone with learnable queries to bridge multimodal understanding and flow-matching generation. To further enhance temporal and semantic alignment, we introduce multi-dimensional Flow-GRPO, a preference post-training strategy for flow-matching models. We include Best-of-N reranked results as AudioMid-BoN.
